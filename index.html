For My Star

A collection of late-night thoughts and feelings I've held onto.
This is our story, told in moments.

The Scent You Leave Behind

Your scent still clings to my shirt, so light—
a quiet echo of our day,
the warmth of arms wrapped soft and tight,
refusing now to fade away.

I press the cloth against my face,
and there you are, though not in sight—
the comfort of your quiet grace,
a hug that lingers through the night.

It smells like laughter, sun, and skin,
like whispered jokes and holding close,
like everything I tuck within
when parting feels like letting go.

Some scents are fleeting, sharp, and gone—
but yours, like home, decides to stay.
And in that trace, I carry on,
wrapped in your love, though far away.

So every time I breathe you in,
I smile, because I know it's true:
this little world upon my skin
means I spent the whole day with you.

Worry, thus Warmth

I’ve been holding this in my chest for a while now, waiting for the right moment to write it down because what happened that day meant more to me than I think you realized.

It was our first real fight...well not really? moreso a small disagreement.

And I know it was small, just a matter of me sleeping in longer than I should have. You were waiting, probably already thinking of what we’d do, hoping I’d reply the moment you messaged. But I didn’t. You watched the minutes stretch too long, maybe feeling let down. Frustrated. Unseen.

When I finally woke up and saw your messages, guilt hit me like a wave. I hated knowing I made you feel like I didn’t care when really, I care so much it aches sometimes. And when we talked, I felt it -that tightness in your voice. The way you were trying to be calm, trying not to snap, but still hurting.

So I cried.
Not because you yelled. You didn’t.
Not because you punished me. You didn’t.
I cried because you didn’t become someone else in your frustration.
Because you stayed you.

You didn’t turn cold. You didn’t mock me or twist the knife with silence. You didn’t make me beg to be forgiven. You listened. You let me speak. You stayed with me in the quiet after the apology, and instead of pushing me away, you laid me down beside you with this quiet tenderness that made my heart break and heal all at once.

It was such a gentle kind of love. One I’m still learning to trust.
Because with Paul, this would’ve gone differently.
He would’ve made me work for forgiveness. He would’ve worn silence like armor and made me peel it off piece by painful piece. There would’ve been jabs disguised as jokes. Cold shoulders. Words meant to make me regret what I did, more than I already did.
But you…

Even in disappointment, you were still a safe place.
Even in hurt, you didn’t let go.
That night I felt something shift.

I realized that being loved by you is not just a feeling, it’s a choice you keep making, even when it’s hard.

A few nights later, we found ourselves side by side again, but this time the air was quieter. Softer. We were past the tears. We were simply dreaming. You asked me what I pictured for our future, and the answers came so naturally it surprised me. We imagined a small apartment filled with warm light and quiet rituals. Wooden floors. Your mug always left half-full on the counter. My paints in the corner. A golden retriever stretched out at our feet and a cat perched wherever it pleases. Our home.

But we were honest. We were both scared. We talked about how hard it might be to get there. How long it might take. How some days the world feels too heavy, too uncertain.

Still, through that worry, we stayed with the vision. Not because we were pretending it would be easy, but because we both believe in the same life. Because we know it’s not just the destination that matters, but who we’re walking with.

Even with all the unknowns, the idea of building something with you feels like a blessing. A quiet promise. We won’t have everything right away. But we’ll have each other. That makes every fear feel softer, every step more bearable.

So this is what I wanted to write down and keep. A reminder of how even in frustration, you showed me grace. And in fear, you gave me hope.
Thank you for loving me the way you do.

Thank you for dreaming with me, even when we’re scared.
And thank you for staying soft, even when things feel heavy.

I love you.
Always.
Moon

That Summer Night I Cried

It finally happened.
The kind of missing that doesn’t just sit quietly in the back of your mind but claws at your chest until it spills out in tears.

I think I understand now—what you meant on the nights you said you missed me so badly it ached. Back then, I loved you, but I hadn’t felt it like that yet. Not in the full, overwhelming, body-heavy way. But this summer gave me no choice. It caught up with me.

Summer class at ADDU was supposed to be just three weeks. It felt short when I first heard it. Just something to get through. But it ended up being some of the hardest, most exhausting days I’ve had in a long time.

I was barely hanging on during the first week. My anxiety was through the roof. I was tired, overstimulated, deprived. Quizzes I thought I prepared for came back with failing marks. And just when I’d usually want to crash into your arms after a long day, I couldn’t.

Home wasn’t even home anymore.
Relatives came to stay, and everything felt like it was closing in. My room was loud and messy. I had no space to breathe, let alone study or rest. Saturdays, the day we always saved for each other, slipped through our fingers because I couldn’t get away.

I missed you every day. I kept texting you that I did, like it was just another thing to say, even though I meant it. But one night, it wasn’t just a message.

I typed “I miss you” like I always did.
Then I broke.

Tears started coming and they didn’t stop. Quiet ones at first, then harder. I wanted you so badly in that moment, not just to talk to, but to hold me. To ground me. To remind me that I wasn’t drowning in all this stress alone. I didn’t want to be brave or okay or strong. I just wanted you.

I needed to feel your arms around me. Your calm breathing. That wordless kind of comfort you give, where you don’t need to fix anything—you just are, and that’s enough.

And it hit me.
This was what you’d felt, maybe more than once.
That deep, physical kind of missing—the kind that fills your throat, blurs your vision, and leaves your heart sore.

I’m late to it.
But I get it now.
And god, it hurts.

I’ve never wanted anything as badly as I wanted to be with you that night. To be held. To feel peace again, even just for a moment. Everything felt so far, so loud, and all I wanted was you.

I cried because I missed my home.
And that’s you.

I love you.
I’ll keep holding on until I’m back in your arms.
Me.

Grace

I woke up late, the sun already down,
your message waiting, quietly there.
But I moved first, stretched, exhaled
not knowing I’d left you in midair.

It wasn’t meant, not in the way that hurts,
just a moment I held for myself.
But you felt the gap, the still reply,
and it sat heavy, all by itself.

You told me you felt left behind,
like a page that someone meant to turn.
And I felt the sting of that,
the way small things can twist and burn.
That night, beneath a quiet sky,

I carried my apology with care.
But I asked, through a tender breath,
“Is this space safe enough to share?”
I said I felt like I can’t misstep,
that even silence shakes your ground

that every pause becomes a weight
and I forget how to be found.
Like if I falter, you might fall,
and I must hold us both alone.

Like loving you means walking tightropes
on threads stretched thin across unknowns.
And you were on the other end
your voice warm through a speaker’s hum.

I couldn’t even turn my cam,
my words came out in sobs, gone numb.
Sniffles broke my every line,
my breath in pieces, slow and rough.

But you stayed soft, you didn’t flinch
your gentleness was just enough.
You said, “I didn’t see it clear.
I didn’t know you felt that way.”

And in that moment, grace arrived
not loud, just choosing me to stay.
There was a pause, the kind that heals,
where nothing needed to be fixed.

Just space to feel, to touch the truth,
to let our shadows gently mix.
You told me then, through tears and thought,
how your emotions take the wheel

how sometimes, spirals drive too fast
before you know what’s false or real.
You said, “I used to give you grace,
without the fear or tight demands.

I used to be your softest place.”
And we both cried, holding hands
Not really, but in the way
two voices tremble into light,

and somehow through a call alone
we still held each other that night.
Because you’re still that—don’t you see?
You’re still my home, my heart, my friend.
And loving you through growing pains
is something I will never end.
Because when you listened—really listened
you forgave me without asking why.
And that kind of love, that kind of peace,
is rare, and deep, and makes me cry.

I don’t resent the cracks we find,
they show we’re real, and raw, and true.
Mistakes will come—but so will grace,
and I’ll keep giving mine to you.

So thank you for the way you stay,
for meeting fear with soft embrace.
You’ll always be my steady light,
my safest home, my quiet place.

You'll always be my steady light,
my safest home, my quiet place.

I love you. Always.

- Your Moon
