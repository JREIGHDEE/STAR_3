The Scent You Leave Behind

Your scent still clings to my shirt, so light—
a quiet echo of our day,
the warmth of arms wrapped soft and tight,
refusing now to fade away.

I press the cloth against my face,
and there you are, though not in sight—
the comfort of your quiet grace,
a hug that lingers through the night.

It smells like laughter, sun, and skin,
like whispered jokes and holding close,
like everything I tuck within
when parting feels like letting go.

So every time I breathe you in,
I smile, because I know it's true:
this little world upon my skin
means I spent the whole day with you.

Worry, thus Warmth

A realization that changed everything.

I’ve been holding this in my chest for a while now... because what happened that day meant more to me than I think you realized.

It was our first real fight... a small disagreement. When I finally woke up, guilt hit me. I hated knowing I made you feel like I didn’t care when really, I care so much it aches sometimes.

So I cried. Not because you yelled. You didn’t. I cried because you didn’t become someone else in your frustration. Because you stayed you.

Even in disappointment, you were still a safe place. Even in hurt, you didn’t let go. That night I felt something shift.

A few nights later... we were simply dreaming. We imagined a small apartment with warm light, wooden floors, a dog, and a cat. Our home.

This is a reminder of how even in frustration, you showed me grace. And in fear, you gave me hope. Thank you for loving me the way you do.

That Summer Night I Cried

When "I miss you" became real.

It finally happened. The kind of missing that claws at your chest until it spills out in tears. Summer class was one of the hardest, most exhausting times.

I was barely hanging on. My anxiety was through the roof. Home wasn’t even home. I had no space to breathe. I missed you every day... but one night, I typed “I miss you” like I always did. Then I broke.

I wanted you so badly in that moment, not just to talk to, but to hold me. To ground me. I didn’t want to be brave or strong. I just wanted you.

I cried because I missed my home.
And that’s you.

And it hit me. This was what you’d felt. That deep, physical kind of missing. I’m late to it. But I get it now. And god, it hurts.

Grace

I woke up late... not knowing I’d left you in midair.

That night, I carried my apology with care.
But I asked, through a tender breath,
“Is this space safe enough to share?”

My breath in pieces, slow and rough.
But you stayed soft, you didn’t flinch
your gentleness was just enough.

You said, “I didn’t see it clear.”
And in that moment, grace arrived
not loud, just choosing me to stay.

Because you’re still that—don’t you see?
You’re still my home, my heart, my friend.

Mistakes will come—but so will grace,
and I’ll keep giving mine to you.

You'll always be my steady light,
my safest home, my quiet place.

I love you. Always.

- Your Moon
